About street-by-street

What this is

Street-by-street (SBS) is a street-level civic audit of Tutrakan, Bulgaria — one street at a time. Each audited street is documented with its physical attributes, open issues, notable assets, and a piece of local history or trivia.

Why it exists

A pothole is data. A pothole that has been there for three years is a pattern. This project tries to look at both — recording what's there, and asking what keeps producing it. The goal is a ground-truth picture of the town that sits alongside official statistics, not in place of them.

How it works

Streets are audited on foot. Photos are captured via Mapillary. Observations are logged as issues (problems) or assets (things of value). Official figures from Bulgaria's National Statistical Institute are included where relevant, always with a source and date.

Status

This is a personal project and a work in progress. Data is incomplete. Unaudited streets show available OpenStreetMap data only.
